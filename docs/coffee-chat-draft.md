*This page runs like an actual conversation, not a curated one. Questions a stranger or a future colleague would really ask, answered the way I'd actually answer them. Grab a coffee. Here's roughly how it goes.*

**First things first. What's your coffee order?**

Whatever's black and doesn't ask questions back. Extra shot if we're deploying today.

**Okay, real question. What do you actually do all day?**

I run engineering at CEM Benchmarking, a Toronto firm that helps pension funds figure out whether they're getting good value from the managers holding their money. I've been there since 2019 and moved into the Director, Engineering seat in the summer of 2026. Day to day it's less code than people assume and more code than my calendar suggests I have time for: sprint planning, cutting releases, a lot of Jira, and owning IT security, which at a firm sitting on pension fund data is not a side project. A colleague once summed the whole thing up as "I run the system around the code," and honestly that's more accurate than my title.

**What's the thing you're proudest of?**

Probably our client data collection platform. I took it from a first scoping conversation to its first live collection cycle with major UK pension funds. Watching something go from "what if we built this" to actually pulling real data from real institutions never gets old.

**You have a reputation for catching things before they break. What's an example?**

This happens more than I'd like to admit. Someone's about to push a release, everyone's confident it's ready, and I'll pull up the branch history out of habit and find a fix everyone swore was already merged, and it just isn't there. Or a data file that looks fine until you line it up against what shipped last cycle and something's shifted two rows over. Nobody catches that by being smart in the moment. You catch it by not trusting your own memory and checking anyway. That's basically become my whole management style: verify, then trust.

**People mention you've gone all in on AI tools. What does that actually look like?**

It's not a novelty act. I use Claude and MCP integrations daily: sprint reporting, documentation, even security audits run through them now. The team's workflow has genuinely changed, not because it's trendy but because it clears out the boring parts of the job fast enough that we spend more time on the parts that actually need a person. It's a tool with judgment attached, and the judgment part is still mine.

**How'd you end up running an engineering team? Doesn't sound like where you started.**

Honestly, no. I started out editing and printing photos in high school. Studied computer science at the University of Toronto Scarborough, then bounced through a graphic design gig, cloud work at Softchoice, and survey programming at a market research firm where I eventually ran the programming team. Landed at CEM in 2019 as a software engineer and just kept getting handed more. First Scrum Master, then team lead, then Director. Nobody plans a path like that. It just compounds.

**What's this I hear about a social committee?**

I started it years ago because the office needed one, and somehow I still run it. A dozen events a year, the holiday party, occasionally a blood drive because someone has to organize that too. It's the least technical thing on my plate and probably the one I'd miss most if I stopped.

**What do you do when you're not staring at a screen?**

Photography mostly. It's made me a better engineer, oddly enough. Composition and interface design use the same eye: you're deciding what the person looking at this should notice first. Beyond that, whisky tasting when someone else is buying, a fair amount of board games, and I've been learning French for a few years with Spanish now creeping in, which mostly means I can order a coffee badly in three languages.

**Okay, real talk. Why is there a hidden cocktail menu on this website?**

Because a resume site shouldn't take itself that seriously. There's a bar page tucked in here somewhere, and the footer will tell you to please drink irresponsibly. There's at least one other thing on this site I'm not going to point out. The label on it is warning enough. If you find them, congratulations, you're exactly the kind of person I'd want to grab a coffee with.

**So if someone actually wanted to get that coffee, how do they find you?**

LinkedIn is the easiest route, email works too. I answer faster than you'd expect from someone running a full sprint calendar and clearing 200 IT tickets a year.

*That's the chat. Coffee's on me if you're ever in Toronto.*
