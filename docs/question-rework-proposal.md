# Question Rework Proposal — "If I were hiring me, what would I ask?"

*Synthesized 2026-07-03 from two hiring-lens audits (recruiter persona + CTO persona), both of
which read the live chat, the resume content, and the colleague-observation working docs.
For Caradec's review. No changes applied. Answers must come from him.*

## Where the two auditors agreed (near-unanimous)

1. **The ratio is the problem, not the questions.** Four of eight current questions carry zero
   hiring signal (coffee order, away from a screen, photography, cats). Both auditors said keep
   them — they make the format work — but substance needs reinforcement.
2. **The verification instinct is the payable differentiator.** The CTO: "the trait that
   prevents the incidents I lose sleep over, and rare enough to pay for." Currently one resume
   bullet; the chat never tells the story.
3. **The autonomy philosophy is hiding in a footnote.** Estimates stay with the engineers;
   he watches for overload. Both auditors called this rare, quotable, and absent.
4. **"Orchestration over authorship" is the Director-level pitch** and appears nowhere.
5. Current questions that earn their place, per both: *what do you do*, *proudest of*,
   *how'd you get here*, and (both flagged it as a quiet winner) *the social committee*.

## Gaps by audience

**The recruiter can't answer:** how big the role is (team size, surface owned), whether he's
even open to being pitched, what would tempt him to move, his management philosophy, the 2018
gap and early-career hops.

**The CTO can't answer:** how he makes technical calls under disagreement, where his scar
tissue is (every current story is a save; "I don't trust a leader with no scar tissue"),
when he puts the keyboard down, how he grows engineers, whether he can land cold in a new org,
how he trades quality against speed.

## REVISED per Caradec (2026-07-03): keep the chat ROLE-AGNOSTIC

His rule: the chat must survive title changes without edits. Questions are about the person
(instincts, philosophy, values), never the current seat. Titles live in The Paperwork, which
is expected to date. The original title-bound proposals are reworked below.

## Proposed additions (role-agnostic; answers need Caradec)

Recommended: add THREE.

1. **"How do you lead without micromanaging?"**
   *Covers:* estimates belong to the people doing the work, watching for overload, leading by
   example. True whether he is a director, a lead, or the most senior person in the room.
2. **"What happens when something's about to ship broken?"**
   *Covers:* THE verification story, anonymized (the stale branch, the shifted data), and the
   habit behind it: check, don't remember. Both auditors independently proposed this. The
   flagship addition, and it is entirely about him, not his title.
3. **"What do you look for in a place to work?"**
   *Covers:* his values, what keeps him somewhere, what kind of problem tempts him. Replaces
   the dated "seven years at one place" tenure question; answers the recruiter's
   what-would-move-him gap without ever needing an update.

**Absorbed rather than added:** the "what does your role mean day to day" question was
title-bound; its substance (orchestration over authorship, the many hats) can live in the
existing "So what do you do?" answer whenever Caradec wants to extend it, or nowhere.

**Bench (role-agnostic already, worthy, not urgent):**
- "Ever shipped something you regretted?" — the boldest trust-builder; needs a real failure
  and what changed. The CTO's top pick; higher risk, higher reward.
- "When do you make yourself put the keyboard down?" — the delegation instinct, phrased to
  work at any seniority.

## Suggested placement

Interleave so charm and substance alternate: coffee order → what do you do → proudest →
**ship broken** → how'd you get here → **what do you look for** → social committee →
**micromanaging** → away from a screen → photography band/Q → cats → Enough small talk →
Paperwork.
